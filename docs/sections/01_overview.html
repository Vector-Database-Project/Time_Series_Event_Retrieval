Overview

Time-series data appears in many domains where signals evolve continuously over time, such as seismic monitoring, physiological sensing, and industrial systems. In many of these settings, the task is not only to classify or predict future behavior, but also to retrieve past events that are similar to a given query event. This project studies event retrieval in time-series data using vector representations, where temporal signal segments are converted into embeddings and indexed for similarity search.

The project uses curated data from EarthScope/IRIS, PhysioNet, and the UCR Time Series Classification Archive. These sources provide organized collections of time-evolving signals with meaningful temporal structure and, in many cases, event-centered or labeled segments. A central part of this work is the comparison of different signal representations, including raw time-domain windows, frequency-domain representations derived from transforms such as FFT and STFT, and mixed time-frequency views. By studying how these representations interact with different embedding approaches across multiple domains, the goal is to identify which choices produce the most effective event retrieval behavior and which combinations are most suitable as a general retrieval policy.
