Overview

Time-series signals arise in seismic monitoring, physiological sensing, and industrial systems, where analysts often need to retrieve past events similar to a query. We treat event retrieval as similarity search by mapping fixed windows to embeddings and querying nearest neighbors in a vector database. Retrieval depends on representation before indexing, raw time windows, frequency features from FFT or STFT, or mixed time–frequency views, and behavior can vary by dataset and event type. We evaluate across EarthScope/IRIS, PhysioNet, and the UCR archive to identify combinations that preserve event similarity and transfer across domains.
