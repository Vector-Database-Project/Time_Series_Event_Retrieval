Potential Results and Discussion

Retrieval performance will be evaluated using raw retrieval counts and ranking-based metrics. For each query at cutoff k, the retrieved set will be analyzed in terms of true positives (TP), false positives (FP), and false negatives (FN). These counts provide a direct view of how many relevant events are successfully retrieved, how many irrelevant events are returned, and how many relevant events are missed.

From these counts, the main top-k retrieval metrics will be computed as:

  • Precision@k, to measure the fraction of retrieved events that are relevant
  • Recall@k, to measure the fraction of relevant events recovered in the retrieved set
  • mean Average Precision (mAP), to evaluate the overall ranking quality of relevant events across the retrieval list
  • nDCG, to evaluate ranking quality under graded relevance, where stronger matches are rewarded more when they appear earlier in the retrieved list

The expected outcome is that retrieval performance will vary across both representation and embedding strategy. Time-only representations may preserve local waveform shape well, while frequency-only representations based on FFT and STFT may better capture periodic or spectral structure. Mixed time-frequency representations are expected to provide stronger retrieval performance in settings where both temporal and spectral characteristics are important. Among the embedding strategies, learned representations such as TS2Vec and InceptionTime are expected to outperform the engineered-feature baseline in some domains, though the strength of this advantage may depend on dataset characteristics and event type.

The final comparison will focus on identifying which representation-method combinations produce the most reliable retrieval behavior within each domain and which combinations generalize most consistently across domains as a practical default retrieval policy.
