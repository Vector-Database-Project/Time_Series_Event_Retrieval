Literature Review and Research Gap

Similar-event retrieval is common in sensor streams (maintenance vibration, IMU, ECG) and often uses metadata filters, so performance depends on embeddings plus indexing and filtered execution behavior.

Time-series representation learning has shifted toward reusable embeddings. TS2Vec shows hierarchical contrastive learning can transfer well for similarity search [1], TNC uses neighborhood consistency under local stationarity assumptions for non-stationary signals [2], and CPC motivates predictive self-supervised objectives across modalities [3].

To anchor comparisons, we include supervised and non-learning baselines. InceptionTime provides a scalable supervised reference and its penultimate features can serve as embeddings when labels exist [4]. tsfresh provides strong engineered descriptors [5], and DTW remains a canonical alignment similarity baseline despite cost [6].

At scale, retrieval relies on ANN indexes and system behavior. HNSW provides strong recall-latency tradeoffs [7], DiskANN targets large regimes with SSD-aware designs [8], Faiss offers a broad indexing taxonomy for controlled comparisons [9], and Milvus highlights system concerns such as updates, distributed execution, and hybrid queries [10]. Filtered ANN results show metadata selectivity can change conclusions [11].

Overall, prior work rarely controls representation view (time, frequency, time–frequency), embedding strategy (self-supervised, supervised, non-learning), and retrieval under filtered workloads within one cross-domain protocol. This motivates our compact evaluation framework to identify encoding and retrieval policies that transfer reliably.
