Potential Results and Discussion

Retrieval performance will be evaluated using raw retrieval counts and ranking-based metrics. For each query at cutoff k, the retrieved set will be analyzed in terms of true positives (TP), false positives (FP), and false negatives (FN). These counts provide a direct view of how many relevant events are successfully retrieved, how many irrelevant events are returned, and how many relevant events are missed.

From these counts, the main top-k retrieval metrics will be computed as:

  • Precision@k, to measure the fraction of retrieved events that are relevant
  • Recall@k, to measure the fraction of relevant events recovered in the retrieved set
  • mean Average Precision (mAP), to evaluate the overall ranking quality of relevant events across the retrieval list
  • nDCG, to evaluate ranking quality under graded relevance, where stronger matches are rewarded more when they appear earlier in the retrieved list

We expect performance to vary by representation (time, FFT or STFT, mixed time–frequency) and embedding approach (TS2Vec, InceptionTime, tsfresh), and we will report which combinations are strongest per domain and which transfer most consistently as a default retrieval policy.
